### Engineering the future of medicine  
**through gene therapy, biomaterials, and spatial immunoengineering**

</div>

---

I am a biomedical engineer developing **gene therapy and mRNA-based therapeutic platforms** to interrogate and reprogram immune and tissue responses in vivo.  
My research integrates **lipid nanoparticle (LNP) engineering**, **spatial proteomics**, and **systems immunology** to understand how tissue context, aging, and biomaterial design jointly shape therapeutic efficacy.

I am currently a **Postdoctoral Researcher in Biomedical Engineering at Duke University**, working with Dr. John W. Hickey, following doctoral and postdoctoral training at Johns Hopkins University.

---

## Research Focus

<div class="three-col">

**Gene Therapy & In Vivo Genome Modulation**  
Design and delivery of nucleic acid and gene-editing payloads using nonviral vectors, with an emphasis on immune and tissue-selective targeting.

**Biomaterials & mRNA Nanomedicine**  
Engineering lipid nanoparticles and biomaterial scaffolds for programmable mRNA delivery, vaccination, and therapeutic immune modulation.

**Spatial Proteomics & Immunoengineering**  
High-dimensional spatial profiling of immune organization and function to guide rational therapeutic and biomaterial design.

</div>
